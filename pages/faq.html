Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about Canadian immigration and our services

What services does CANVEX Immigration provide?

+

We provide comprehensive guidance and administrative support for various Canadian immigration pathways, including permanent residence (Express Entry, PNP, Family Sponsorship), study permits, work permits, and business immigration. We help with eligibility assessment, application preparation, document organization, and submission support.

Do you guarantee visa approval?

+

No, we do not guarantee visa approval. All immigration decisions are made solely by Canadian immigration authorities (IRCC). We provide guidance and support to help you prepare complete and accurate applications based on current regulations. Final approval depends on your individual circumstances, meeting all requirements, and immigration officer discretion.

Are you qualified to provide immigration services?

+

Yes, our team consists of experienced Canadian immigration professionals who provide immigration advice and representation. We operate in full compliance with Canadian immigration regulations and professional standards.

How long does the immigration process take?

+

Processing times vary significantly depending on the program, your country of residence, application completeness, and current IRCC processing volumes. Express Entry applications typically take 6-8 months, while other programs may take 12-24 months or longer. We provide realistic timelines based on current processing standards during your consultation.

What is the Express Entry system?

+

Express Entry is Canada's online system for managing applications for permanent residence from skilled workers. It includes three programs: Federal Skilled Worker Program, Federal Skilled Trades Program, and Canadian Experience Class. Candidates are ranked using the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS) based on factors like age, education, work experience, and language proficiency.

Can I bring my family with me to Canada?

+

Yes, in most cases you can include your spouse or common-law partner and dependent children in your permanent residence application. For temporary permits (study/work), family members may be eligible for accompanying permits. We can help you understand the specific requirements for including family members in your application.

What documents do I need to prepare?

+

Required documents vary by program but commonly include: passport, birth certificate, marriage certificate (if applicable), educational credentials and transcripts, language test results (IELTS/CELPIP), work experience letters, police clearance certificates, medical exam results, and proof of funds. We provide a comprehensive document checklist specific to your pathway during consultation.

How much does it cost to immigrate to Canada?

+

Costs include government application fees (ranging from $1,000-$2,500 CAD for most programs), language tests ($200-$300 CAD), educational credential assessments ($200-$500 CAD), medical exams ($200-$400 CAD), police certificates (varies), and proof of funds requirements. Our consultation fees are separate and discussed during your initial assessment.

What is a Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)?

+

Provincial Nominee Programs allow Canadian provinces and territories to nominate individuals who want to immigrate to Canada and settle in a specific province. Each province has its own criteria and streams targeting specific skills, work experience, or business investment. A provincial nomination can significantly boost your Express Entry CRS score.

How do I access my client portal?

+

We provide regular updates on your application status through direct communication with our team. You'll receive timely information about your case progress and any additional requirements.
